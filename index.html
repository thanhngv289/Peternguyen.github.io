About Me

I am a researcher in Chemical Engineering with interests in the design and development of functional nanomaterials for energy, environmental, and biomedical applications. My work focuses on advanced electrode materials and interfacial engineering to improve electrochemical performance in systems such as sensing, capacitive deionization, and energy storage.

Research Interests:
  • MXene-based nanomaterials and surface functionalization
  • Conducting polymer composites (PANI/MXene)
  • Electrochemical sensing (biomedical & environmental detection)
  • Capacitive deionization (CDI) and desalination technologies
  • Energy storage materials (supercapacitors)

Research

1. MXene-Based Electrode Engineering

My research involves engineering MXene nanosheets through surface modification, interlayer design, and composite formation with conductive polymers to improve ion transport, stability, and electrochemical activity.

2. Capacitive Deionization (CDI) for Water Treatment

I investigate advanced electrode materials for CDI systems, targeting efficient removal of ions such as Na+, Cl-, and NH4+. My approach includes functionalizing MXenes and integrating redox-active components to enhance selectivity and capacity.

3. Electrochemical Biosensing for Cancer Detection

I am currently focusing on developing electrochemical sensors for biomedical applications, including detection of breast cancer biomarkers. This work aims to integrate nanomaterial interfaces with high sensitivity and rapid response.

Keywords: MXene, PANI, electrochemical sensors, CDI, supercapacitors, nanocomposites
